## Special rules by domain

Certain areas of law impose additional constraints on interpretation:

- **Criminal law.** Strict construction prevails (nulla poena sine lege stricta). Interpretation must not extend criminal liability beyond what the text clearly covers. Analogical extension to the detriment of the accused is prohibited. Vagueness may invalidate a penal provision.
- **Tax law.** Obligations to pay tax must have a clear legal basis. Tax exemptions and reliefs are generally construed strictly.
- **Fundamental rights.** Rights provisions should be interpreted broadly to ensure effective protection; limitations on rights should be interpreted narrowly and must satisfy the proportionality test.
- **International treaties.** Interpret in good faith, in accordance with the ordinary meaning of terms in their context and in light of the treaty's object and purpose (VCLT Art. 31). Supplementary means, including preparatory work and circumstances of conclusion, may be used to confirm meaning or resolve ambiguity (VCLT Art. 32). For multilingual treaties, where versions diverge, prefer the meaning that best reconciles them in light of the treaty's object and purpose (VCLT Art. 33).
- **Framework instruments.** In broad, purpose-driven texts (e.g. EU founding treaties, constitutional framework statutes), objective and contextual interpretation carries particular weight because the text is intentionally drafted in open-textured, goal-oriented language.

## Scholarly foundations

The methodology above synthesises principles from three works
spanning different legal traditions. It is an original
integration designed for cross-jurisdictional use, not a
reproduction of any single source.

1. **Barak, _Purposive Interpretation in Law_** (2005): the
   three-component framework (language, purpose, discretion)
   and the calibration of subjective vs. objective purpose by
   text type.
2. **Lenaerts & Gutiérrez-Fons, "To Say What the Law of the
   EU Is"** (2014): multilingual interpretation, effet utile,
   and teleological methods in supranational legal orders.
3. **Wintr, _Metody a zásady interpretace práva_** (2013):
   systematic catalogue of interpretive principles across the
   four classical methods, including collision rules and
   proportionality analysis.
