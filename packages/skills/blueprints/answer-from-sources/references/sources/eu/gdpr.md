# GDPR — Regulation (EU) 2016/679

<!-- guide: A per-source orientation note: what the source is, when it binds, which parts this skill uses most, and the traps. Keep it short — the assistant opens it to orient, not to read the whole act. Write one such note per source you add. -->

- Directly applicable in every EU member state; no national transposition needed. Member states may still legislate where the regulation leaves room (notably Art. 8 on the age of consent, Art. 23 restrictions, and Arts. 85–91) — check national law when a question touches those areas.
- Provisions this skill reaches for most: Art. 5 (principles), Art. 6 (lawful bases), Arts. 12–22 (data-subject rights), Arts. 24–28 (controller and processor duties), Arts. 33–34 (breach notification).
- Cite by article and paragraph, e.g. "Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR".
