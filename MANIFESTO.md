# Stella Manifesto

Stella is a legal workspace: free to use, open to inspect, yours to keep.

We are building it because we believe the craft of legal practice deserves
infrastructure built with the same care.

## Our Principles

### Future is open.

Our code is open source, our roadmap is public and our work is transparent.
You can inspect, audit, and contribute to it. You can self-host it, or integrate
with the tools you already use. Join us, or fork it and make it your own.

### Clarity, not magic.

Every part of Stella should be understandable: how it works, what it costs, why it does
what it does. AI-powered flows are grounded by citations and traceable to
source material. If something is not clear, that is our problem to fix, not yours to
accept.

### Fair pricing.

Matter management, document storage, time tracking, invoicing: free. You pay for AI.
Prices are on the website because we do not have secret ones.

### Your practice, your data.

We do not lock you in. Your documents are standard files, not proprietary formats,
stored in systems you can easily export from or switch to self-hosting. As the sector
evolves, we adopt open standards as they emerge.

### Built to last.

Every operation in Stella (opening a matter, searching documents, reviewing documents)
can be performed by a person, a script, or by an AI agent. Sometimes typing a
sentence is just faster than clicking through five screens: we build for that, and for
whatever comes next. The instrument should outlast the trend.

### Responsible by design.

The legal profession has ethical obligations that predate software by centuries.
We design around them from the start.

If any of this resonates, we would like to hear from you.
