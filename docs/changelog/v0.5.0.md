# Document templates, grouped tables, and right-to-left support

## stella turns documents into reusable templates, groups table rows into sections, and now reads right-to-left.

Turn a finished document into a template you can reuse. stella spots the parts that change, suggests names for them, and marks them in the document. Add rules so clauses only show up when they should, and fields that fill themselves in. When you need a fresh copy, fill in the blanks (or just ask stella in chat) and it writes the final document for you.

Big tables are easier to make sense of now. Group rows by any column to fold a long list into tidy sections, select several at once and act on them together, and see how many rows match a value before you filter by it. Where stella fills in a cell for you, a hover shows exactly where the answer came from.

stella now reads right-to-left. Switch the language to Arabic and the whole app flips to match: menus, tables, the editor, and chat. Arabic and English sit together without scrambling, dates and numbers look right in each language, and legal terms stay consistent throughout.

A few more things: build and edit skills right inside stella with a guided setup; chats suggest what to ask next and catch you up when you come back later; and the document editor gains drag-to-resize images, fields and tables of contents that update themselves, and Markdown export.

Once again, a big thank you to the community for the contributions.
