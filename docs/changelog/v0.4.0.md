# Document translation, business registries and longer chats

## Translate documents in place, look up companies across ten jurisdictions, and keep chats going further

Pick a target language from the document viewer or the editor's action bar, and stella, using DeepL as the first translation option, produces a translated copy as a new file in the same matter, ready to read, edit, and compare against the original. An org-level DeepL key switches it on for the whole workspace.

<video controls src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f1e394a7-d109-4029-bc20-3cf9ea17b5e3"></video>

stella also knows a lot more about companies: business-registry lookups now cover ten jurisdictions — Czech ARES, Slovak ORSR, Polish KRS, Finnish PRH, French recherche-entreprises, Norwegian Brreg, UK Companies House, US SEC EDGAR, Taiwanese GCIS, and EU VIES VAT validation — all behind a single jurisdiction-aware lookup tool in chat. And chats run longer and steadier: history compaction keeps context within budget while loop detection stops runaway exchanges before they spiral.

Rounding out the release: prompts, skills, and connectors are unified under one Knowledge tools surface, and the built-in .docx editor took a large step up in fidelity with watermarks, block-level content controls, DrawingML shapes, OMML math, and broader page-border and image coverage.

We are still early days, but community issues, PRs, and questions are coming in. If solving legal problems with software is your thing, the next stretch is open to help.
